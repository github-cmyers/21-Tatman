⚠️ Comparable-Supported Value: $635,000 – $660,000

🌎 Macroeconomic & Global Risk Factors

The property-level risks above exist within a broader macroeconomic environment that amplifies the downside of this investment. The following global conditions increase the risk profile beyond what any inspection or income analysis can capture.

📈 Rising Interest Rates
Rate Risk
A 1% rate increase on a $528k loan adds ~$310/mo to carrying costs. Rate volatility compresses margins on an already tight investment. Refinancing risk is real if rates don’t decline.
🚨 Rising Unemployment
Tenant Risk
Unemployment increases tenant default risk and vacancy rates. Worcester’s rental market is already under stress. A single missed month on a margin-thin multi-family creates immediate out-of-pocket exposure for the owner.
⚡ Rising Energy Costs
OpEx Risk
Landlord-paid water and sewer costs are already factored in, but rising energy costs increase tenant burden and can strain rent affordability. Higher utility bills reduce tenants’ effective ability to pay — and can drive turnover in a tight budget household.
📉 Potential Recession
Market Risk
A recession typically softens property values, compresses rents, and extends vacancy cycles. For a property purchased at the top of its comp range, a 5–10% decline in market value would erode the down payment equity and extend the time horizon to break even significantly.
Bottom Line on Macro Risk Even at $660k, this property has thin margins. Any one of these external pressures — alone or in combination — amplifies the risk for an investor entering at the top of the comp range.
