🌎 Macroeconomic & Global Risk Factors

The property-level risks above exist within a broader macroeconomic environment that amplifies the downside of this investment. The following global conditions increase the risk profile beyond what any inspection or income analysis can capture.

📈 Rising Interest Rates
Rate Risk
A 1% rate increase on a $560k loan adds ~$330/mo to carrying costs. This property already produces negative cash flow at 6.32% — any rate movement makes the math worse. Refinancing risk is real.
🚨 Rising Unemployment
Tenant Risk
Unemployment increases tenant default risk and vacancy rates. Worcester’s rental market is already under stress. A single missed month on this margin-thin property creates immediate out-of-pocket exposure with no cash reserve buffer.
⚡ Rising Energy Costs
OpEx Risk
Landlord-paid water and sewer costs are already factored in, but rising energy costs increase tenant burden and can strain rent affordability. Higher utility bills reduce tenants’ effective ability to pay — and can drive turnover in a tight budget household.
📉 Potential Recession
Market Risk
A recession typically softens property values, compresses rents, and extends vacancy cycles. For a property that is already cash-flow negative at purchase, a 5–10% decline in market value would erode the down payment equity and extend the time horizon to break even significantly.
Bottom Line on Macro Risk This property has zero margin for error at $700k. Any one of these external pressures — alone or in combination — turns a manageable investment into a sustained financial loss.
