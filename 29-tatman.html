Professional Inspection Not Conducted
No inspection report is available. A 117-year-old property will almost certainly have deferred maintenance items (roof, foundation, electrical, plumbing, chimney) that could represent $10,000–$30,000+ in additional costs.
Individual Unit Rent Breakdown
The listing only discloses total gross rent of $5,050/mo. Individual unit rents, lease terms, and tenant payment history are unknown. Some tenants may be below market, creating upside — or may have unfavorable lease terms.
Roof, Foundation & Major Systems
The listing does not mention roof age, foundation condition, electrical panel status, plumbing material (lead, copper, PEX), or water heater ages. These are critical cost factors on a 1909 property.
Lead Paint & Asbestos
Built in 1909, lead paint and asbestos are highly likely present unless previously abated. Massachusetts law requires de-leading for properties with children under 6. Remediation costs can be substantial ($5,000–$20,000+ per unit).
Landlord-Paid vs Tenant-Paid Utilities
The listing does not clearly state which utilities the landlord pays. If the owner is responsible for heat, electric, or water/sewer, annual operating expenses could be significantly higher than estimated.
~11 Months on Market + $100k Price Cut
Listed May 2025, reduced from $799,900 to $699,900. Extended time on market and a significant price reduction signal that the original asking was not supported by buyer demand. The seller may be motivated to negotiate further.
